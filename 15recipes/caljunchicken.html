Cajun Chicken Sandwiches with Roasted Potato Wedges & Secret Sauce

step 1

Preheat oven to 425 degrees. Wash and dry produce. Cut potatoes into ½-inch-thick wedges. Trim and peel onion; cut crosswise into ½-inch-thick rounds. Halve buns. Toss potatoes on a baking sheet with a drizzle of oil, salt, and pepper. Roast until crispy, 20-25 minutes.

step 2

In a small bowl, combine 4 TBSP mayonnaise, 1 TBSP ketchup, 1 tsp hot sauce, 1 tsp water, ½ tsp Blackening Spice, ¼ tsp sugar, and a pinch of salt and pepper. (For 4 servings, use 8 TBSP mayonnaise, 2 TBSP ketchup, 2 tsp hot sauce, 2 tsp water, 1 tsp Blackening Spice, and ½ tsp sugar.)

step 3

Pat chicken dry with paper towels; place between 2 large pieces of plastic wrap. Pound with a mallet or rolling pin until chicken is about ½ inch thick. Season all over with remaining Blackening Spice, salt, and pepper. Heat a drizzle of oil in a medium pan over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, 4-6 minutes per side. (Reduce heat if chicken begins to brown too quickly.) Transfer to a plate to rest.

step 4

IHeat a drizzle of oil in same pan over medium-high heat. Add onion rounds and cook until deeply browned and tender, 2-3 minutes per side. Turn off heat; transfer to a plate. Wipe out pan.

step 5

Melt 1 TBSP butter (2 TBSP for 4 servings) in pan used for onion rounds over medium heat. Add buns, cut sides down, to hot pan. Toast until golden brown, 1-2 minutes. (Depending on the size of your pan, you may need to work in batches.)

step 6

Spread cut sides of each toasted bun with 1 TBSP secret sauce. Fill buns with chicken and onion. Divide sandwiches and potato wedges between plates. Serve with remaining secret sauce on the side for dipping.Chicken is fully cooked when internal temperature reaches 165°.
